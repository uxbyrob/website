Z-axis

By combining the idea of spaces and layers, more work can be done on a page. The advantage of this to the user, is ability to get more done without losing their sense of place and interrupting the work-flow. Large panels can fly in for sub-tasks or small growlers can float in to notify a user of a background action. Layers can also be combined with dynamic panels to expand a piece of content or work-flow.

 

Shadows are set in 3 depths. Items with the same shadow level will "push" each other out of the way versus overlaying an object with a lower shadow level. Shadows are also in indication to the user which elements on the page are movable and which are "stuck in place".

 

Shadow 1:

Use for visual emphasis

Indicate a panel is movable by the user - Example left rail

 

Shadow 2:

Use for panels that can move over content with shadow 1 applied - Example right subtask panel.

 

Shadow 3:

This is the highest level and will sit above all page element. Modals and overlays still sit higher than shadow 3 objects in the Z order.
