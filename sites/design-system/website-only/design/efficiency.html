Efficiency

Hicks law describes the time it takes for a person to make a decision as a result of the possible choices he or she has. In this design system, we protect the user's time by providing the right information and controls at the right time and right place. The measurement of a successful visit is not time on site but rather efficiency and accuracy of work-flow completion.

 

 

Efficiency design tools:

  • Progressive Reveal
    • Decrease cognitive load by showing only what is needed when it's needed

 

  • In-line
    • Whenever possible, use morphing and motion to keep interactions in-line and maintain the users sense of place. Reducing modals and page changes will create a faster and less jarring experience

 

  • Make the simple easy. Make the complex possible.
    • Visible controls and options should be limited to essential and frequent tasks. Non-essential, low use and infrequent controls and settings should be removed from the primary UI and moved to a drawer or secondary section.
    • Reduce the friction within work flows.  When possible remove confirmations and replace them with undo's.

 

  • Placement of buttons should be at the natural end of a process and not in a set place on the page. Buttons should always appear with the cancel or negative to the left of the primary action.

 

  • Object oriented design (patterns)
    • Whenever possible, patterns in the UI should be created and reused. Creating patterns reduces the cognitive load by helping users predict how an interaction will function.

 

  • Whitespace
    • White or negative space increases comprehension of content and increases the speed of decisions. In this system, clutter is the enemy and filler elements such as double gutters, low use controls and unneeded elements must be removed.

Clutter is the enemy

Make the simple, easy. Make the complex, possible.

 

Creating a cluttered interface is the easy way out.

Taking time to prioritize functions and understanding that for every new element placed on a page, the access to every other element is diluted.
