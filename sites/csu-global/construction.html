Construction Management combines the art and science of building technology with essential principles of business, management, computer technology and leadership.

The job of a Construction Manager is to transform the design of a project from paper to reality by taking a set of written plans and specifications and a raw piece of land and coordinating all of the materials, manpower, and equipment to guarantee the set price, schedule and quality.

The goal of the Construction Management program is to prepare graduates with the necessary skills in:

  • Estimating project costs and budgets
  • Planning and scheduling project progress and timetables
  • Administering the contract
  • Managing job site and operations by coordinating onsite activities
  • Managing project quality
  • Managing project safety

A bachelor's degree in Construction Management from CSU-Pueblo Online will help students who have foundations in Construction Management from an Associate's degree to quickly earn a Bachelor's degree*.

Courses in this program include:

  • CET 303: Construction Management
  • CET 304: Building Cost Estimating
  • CM 445: Construction Safety
  • CM 231: Statics and Structures

Job outlook:

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics Construction Managers is expected to grow 16 percent from 2012 to 2022, faster than the average for all occupations.

As overall construction activity expands, population and businesses grow, and buildings are retrofitted to be more energy efficient, the need for qualified individuals to oversee these projects will grow. As opportunities for construction managers grow, candidates with a bachelor's degree will have the best job prospects.
